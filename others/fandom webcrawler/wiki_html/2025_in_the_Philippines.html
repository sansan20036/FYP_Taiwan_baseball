Holidays

Further information: Public holidays in the Philippines

In August 2024, the Senate discussed reducing holidays to boost productivity during the remaining sessions of Congress, with a focus on legislation addressing the number of holidays from different cities, municipalities, provinces including those of national level (besides religious) in the country.[61]

On October 31, the national government publicly released the list of holidays and non-working days, which are indicated by proclamations signed by the president a day earlier. Proclamation No. 727, series of 2024, contains the general list,[62] with the People Power anniversary, which was not declared a holiday for 2024, being returned but as a working day.[63] A day prior to All Saints' Day (October 31) is included in the list, while All Souls' Day (November 2) is excluded.[63][64]

Meanwhile, Proclamation No. 729, s. 2024, declared July 27 as a special non-working day in commemoration of the founding anniversary of the Iglesia ni Cristo.[65]
