    In an effort to counter Ukraine’s 2024 offensive in Kursk, in early 2025 Russian forces launched cross-border incursions from Russia into Ukraine.

    On 10 January 2025, Russian troops crossed the international border into Ukraine’s Sumy Oblast, advancing west of Zhuravka.[2] However, a few days later, the Ukrainian military claimed their forces had successfully pushed back the Russian attack.

    In mid-February, Russian forces launched another cross-border incursion,[3][4][5][6] claiming to have captured the village of Basivka,[7] followed by Novenke a few days later.[8]

    By the end of February, Ukraine's Center for Combatting Disinformation acknowledged the presence of Russian forces, while DeepState described Basivka and Novenke as "gray zones."[9][10]

    Background

    [edit]
    Main articles: Russian occupation of Sumy Oblast and Battle of Sumy

    Throughout 2024, Russian forces had launched limited cross-border attacks in Sumy Oblast, targeting the villages of Zhuravka, Chuikivka, Rozhkovychi, and Sytne, but all attempts ended in failure.[11][12] Russian troops raided the village of Ryzhivka in June 2024, located near the border, close to the Russian settlement of Tyotkino. Geolocation footage confirmed that Russian troops had entered the village and advanced approximately 730 meters into Ukraine.[13] However, the next day, Russian forces withdrew from the area.[14]

    On 6 August 2024, Ukrainian forces launched an offensive into Russia's Kursk Oblast and occupied part of the region. On 15 August, they captured the town of Sudzha. Around the same time, Ukrainian military commander Oleksandr Syrskyi announced the establishment of a military administration in the occupied areas of Kursk Oblast, which would be led by Major General Eduard Moskaliov as the head of the military command office.

    Opposing forces

    [edit]

    Russia

    [edit]

    The 810th Naval Infantry Brigade was the first Russian unit known to have entered the Sumy Oblast when it was reported in mid-February that some of its units had crossed the border and entered the village of Basivka.[15][16] Amid their operations in Sumy Oblast throughout February and March, other elements of the 810th Brigade have remained in the area of Guyevo, Plekhovo, and Kurilovka [ru] in the Kursk Oblast.[17][18][19][20]

    By early March, it was widely reported that the 83rd Air Assault Brigade, which had taken the Russian border village of Nikolayevo-Darino in January,[21] was operating around the neighboring village of Zhuravka [uk] in Sumy Oblast,[22][23][24][25] where it remained throughout the month.[26][27]

    By 22 February, it had been reported that the 56th Air Assault Regiment was operating near the border village of Novenke, in Sumy Oblast.[28] Throughout March 2025, it was reported that units of the regiment, including its drone units,[29] were operating within Sumy Oblast and along the international border.[30][31][32]

    By late March, it was reported that the 51st Airborne Regiment [uk], which had been located in the Russian border villages of Sverdlikovo [ru] and Lebedevka [ru] the previous month,[16][28][17] had taken control of most of Basivka[31] and was present near the Sudzha border post.[32]

    It was reported in late March that elements of the 34th Mountain Motor Rifle Brigade had advanced near the Ukrainian border village of Veselivka,[27] after having taken part in an operation to capture the village of Malaya Loknya in Kursk Oblast earlier in the month.[20]

    On April 5, it was reported that the "Tigers" drone detachment of the 83rd Airborne Brigade were using fibre optic FPV drones at the Kursk-Sumy border.[33]

    On April 6, ISW reported at elements of the Russian 34th Motorized Rifle Brigade are operating near the town of Demidovka.[34]

    Ukraine

    [edit]

    Units of Ukraine's 36th Marine Brigade repelled a Russian assault (which they claimed involved North Korean forces) between Sverdlikovo and Novenke in mid-March 2025.[35] The 36th Brigade was still in defense of the border villages of Sumy Oblast by the end of March 2025.[36]

    By late March, the 103rd Territorial Defense Brigade was operating in a border village in Sumy Oblast.[37]

    Ukraine's 78th Airborne Assault Regiment repelled a Russian attempt to cross the state border near the village of Zhuravka in February 2025.[38]

    Throughout March, the 15th Mobile Border Detachment of Ukraine's State Border Guard Service engaged in combat with Russian forces on the border of the Sumy Oblast.[39][40]

    Incursion

    [edit]

    On February 19, 2025, Russian President Vladimir Putin announced on a televised broadcast that the 810th Brigade had crossed the border between the Russian Federation and Ukraine, entering Sumy Oblast.[4][5][6] However, this claim was denied by the head of Ukraine's Center for Combatting Disinformation, Andriy Kovalenko, as well as senior Ukrainian military officials.[41][42][43][44][45]

    The following day, Russian sources reported that soldiers from the 810th Brigade had successfully entered Basivka, while Ukrainian sources confirmed that Russian forces had stormed the border in the Sumy Oblast.[7][3] Later, on 25 February, Russian forces again claimed that they had seized Novenke and were making progress toward northern Zhuravka [uk].[8] Meanwhile, the Ukrainian military acknowledged that Russian troops had crossed the border near Novenke but stated that they had successfully pushed them back into Russian territory.[46]

    On 28 February, Ukraine's Center for Combatting Disinformation acknowledged that Russian forces were attacking along the international border near Basivka and Novenke. Meanwhile, DeepState described both Basivka [uk] and Novenke as "grey zones".[9][10] By March 2, Russian military bloggers claimed that Russian forces had captured Zhuravka.[47] Around the same time, DeepState reported that Russian troops had broken through near the settlements of Novenke and Zhuravka and were reinforcing their positions.[48] On 7 March 2025, Russian sources claimed that their armed forces had taken over Novenke,[49] and the next day, based on geolocation footage released, it’s likely that Russian forces captured Novenke.[50][51]

    After successfully recapturing the town of Sudzha, Russian President Vladimir Putin proposed establishing a "buffer zone" in Sumy Oblast to protect Kursk from future attacks.[52] Starting on 11 March, Russian forces advanced west of Basivka and southwest of Zhuravka.[53] in an effort to consolidate their positions in the region.[54][55][56] A Ukrainian official claimed that Russia was massing troops along the border in preparation for an "assault" on Sumy Oblast.[57]

    By 23 March, Russian sources claimed their troops had moved into Volodymyrivka [uk].[58] A few days later, geolocated footage indicated that Russian forces had seized the border checkpoint between Sumy and Sudzha to the east of Basivka.[59][60] Ukrainian commander Syrskyi said that their forces are conducting a defensive operation to prevent Russian troops from advancing further into the northern Sumy region.[61]

    At the end of March, geolocation footage showed that Russian forces captured Veselivka,[62] and the Russian Ministry of Defense also claimed that Russian troops had taken control of Veselivka.[63][64] However, Ukrainian officials denied the reports of the Russian breakthrough.[65][66]

    On 6 April, footage showed Russian troops advancing south of Basivka.[67] At the same time, Russia’s Defense Minister claimed they had already seized Basivka.[68][69][67]

    Casualties

    [edit]

    Civilian casualties

    [edit]

    On 12 March, Russia carried out mortar and artillery attacks on the Myropillia hromada, injuring an adult man.[70]

    On 22 March, Ukrainian media reported that the Ukraine's State Emergency Service evacuated 31 people—including three children—from the border area of Sumy Oblast.[71] On 30 March, while Ukraine's State Emergency Service was evacuating civilians from the Sumy border region, Russian forces launched an attack in the area, leaving one man injured.[72]

    Military casualties

    [edit]

    Russian

    [edit]

    On 12 March, the spokesperson for the State Border Guard Service, Andrii Demchenko, reported that around 20 Russian soldiers were killed near the border area, while approximately 15 others were wounded.[73]
