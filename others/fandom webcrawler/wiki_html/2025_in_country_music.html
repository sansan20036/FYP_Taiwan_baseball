  • January 20 – Carrie Underwood performs "America the Beautiful" during the swearing-in ceremony at the second inauguration of Donald Trump.[1]
  • January 28 – Trace Adkins' 2003 hit "Hot Mama" became controversial when it was performed by South Dakota Governor and homeland secretary, Kristi Noem, after walking out for DHS remarks to it, resulting allegations on the campaign's attempt to objectify women. This incident has been referred to as sexist.[2]
  • March 19 – The Grand Ole Opry celebrated its 100th birthday with a concert special hosted by Blake Shelton which featured over 50 of the Opry's living members in attendance.[3]
  • March 31 – Orville Peck will make his Broadway debut in Cabaret, taking over the role of the Emcee from Adam Lambert for a limited sixteen show run at the August Wilson Theatre. Peck will star alongside two-time Tony Award nominee Eva Noblezada as Sally Bowles.[4]
  • April 25–27 – The 2025 edition of Stagecoach Festival takes place at Empire Polo Club in Indio, California, featuring headline performances from Zach Bryan, Jelly Roll, and Luke Combs.[5]

Deaths

[edit]
  • January 1 – Chad Morgan, 91, Australian country singer and guitarist[64]
  • January 13 – Buck White, 94, American bluegrass instrumentalist, member of the Whites[65]
  • January 15 – Melba Montgomery, 86, American country singer ("No Charge")[66]
  • January 24 – Buddy Brock, 72, American songwriter ("You've Got to Stand for Something", "Watermelon Crawl")[67]
  • March 6 – Troy Seals, 86, American songwriter ("Who's Gonna Fill Their Shoes", "Seven Spanish Angels")[68]
  • March 20 – Eddie Adcock, 86, American bluegrass banjo player (The Country Gentlemen)[69]
  • March 29 – Dick Damron, 91, Canadian singer and songwriter[70]
  • April 1 – Johnny Tillotson, 86, American singer-songwriter of the 1960s, best known for "It Keeps Right On a-Hurtin'"[71]
