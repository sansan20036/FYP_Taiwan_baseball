Events in the year 2025 in Norway.

Incumbents

[edit]
  • Monarch – Harald V
  • President of the Storting – Masud Gharahkhani (Labour).
  • Prime Minister – Jonas Gahr Støre (Labour).

Events

[edit]
  • 8 January – 2 February – The 2025 World Men's Handball Championship in Croatia, Denmark and Norway.[1]
  • 4 February – Former prime minister Jens Stoltenberg is appointed as finance minister in the Støre Cabinet after his predecessor, Trygve Slagsvold Vedum and his Centre Party withdraw from the coalition government.[2] This results in Norway's first single-party government since 2001.
  • 24 March – A trial against athletics coach Gjert Ingebrigtsen for alleged domestic violence commences in Sør-Rogaland District Court.[3]
  • 31 March – The first instance of an orbital rocket launched from Europe (excluding Russia), by Isar Aerospace from Andøya Spaceport, lasts for about 40 seconds before it crashes into the sea.[4]

Scheduled

[edit]
  • Norway aims to ban the sale of all new diesel and petrol cars by this year.[5][6]
  • 8 September – 2025 Norwegian parliamentary election[7][8]

Anniversaries

[edit]
  • 13 March – 200 years since the birth of Hans Gude.[9]
  • 10 June – 200 years since the birth of Sondre Norheim.[10]
  • 29 June – 100 years since the death of Christian Michelsen.[11]
  • 1 October – 200 years since the foundation stone was laid for the Royal Palace, Oslo.[12]
  • 16 October – 100 years since the death of Christian Krohg.[13]

Sports

[edit]
  • 5 January – Johannes Høsflot Klæbo and Therese Johaug win the 2024–25 Tour de Ski for men and women respectively.
  • 13 January – Three-time Champions League winner Vipers Kristiansand declares bankruptcy and is evicted from the 2024–25 handball league.[15]
  • 22 January – The member clubs of Norsk Toppfotball vote (19 to 13) to work for the abolishment of video assistant refereeing.[16]
  • 15 February – The 2025 Nordic Junior World Ski Championships concludes, with Norway atop the medal table, among others with seven out of seven possible gold medals in cross-country skiing (U20).
  • 26 February–9 March – The FIS Nordic World Ski Championships 2025 is held in Trondheim.
  • Norway wins 14 gold medals and finishes first in the medal table. Johannes Høsflot Klæbo wins every single race in the men's cross-country skiing.
  • 1 March – The national convention of the Football Association of Norway votes to retain video assistant refereeing.[17]
  • 2 March – The national convention of the Football Association of Norway votes to stage the Norwegian football cup finals for men and women in the spring rather than in the late fall, as has been tradition since its inception.[18]
  • 6–9 March – Norway takes three gold medals and finishes third in the medal table at the 2025 European Athletics Indoor Championships.
  • 9 March – The director of sports in the elite ski jumping section of the Norwegian Ski Federation, Jan Erik Aalbu, publicly admits that the ski jumping team illegally tampered with their gear to enhance performances.[19]
  • 9 March – FK Bodø/Glimt advance to the quarter-final of the 2024–25 UEFA Europa League, as Kasper Høgh concurrently moves into position as top goalscorer of the tournament.[20]
  • 13–16 March – The 2025 World Single Distances Speed Skating Championships is held in Hamar.
  • Norway wins three gold medals and finishes third in the medal table.
  • 16 March – Jarl Magnus Riiber races for the last time in Nordic combined.[21]
  • 21–23 March – Norway takes three gold medals and finishes second in the medal table at the 2025 World Athletics Indoor Championships.
  • Jakob Ingebrigtsen took Norway's first gold medal at the World Athletics Indoor Championships since 1995, and Ingebrigtsen became the first male athlete from any country to win back-to-back gold medals at the World Athletics Indoor Championships since 1999.[22]
  • 23 March – Tarjei Bø and Johannes Thingnes Bø races for the last time in biathlon.[23]

[edit]
  • 11 February – Knut Flatin, painter and printmaker (born 1945).[46][47]
  • 12 February – Barthold Halle, stage instructor, film director and theatre director (born 1925).[48]
  • 21 February – Alf Bakke, entomologist and forest researcher (born 1927).[49][better source needed]

    • Bjørn Ludvigsen, footballer (born 1969).[50]
