This article summarizes the events related to rock music for the year of 2025.

Notable events

[edit]

January

[edit]
  • Scottish rock band Franz Ferdinand releases their sixth studio album, The Human Fear, their first in seven years, following Always Ascending (2018). It charts in the top 10 of 5 national album charts, including topping the Scottish albums chart.[1]
  • Many rock bands perform at FireAid, a two day benefit concert to help raise funds for those affected by the January 2025 Southern California wildfires. Performers include Green Day, Red Hot Chili Peppers, No Doubt and Alanis Morissette.[2]
  • Nirvana also reforms for a surprise brief performance at FireAid. The lineup included bassist Krist Novoselic, drummer Dave Grohl, rhythm guitarist Pat Smear, and four separate female vocalists in place of frontman Kurt Cobain. St. Vincent performed the song "Breed", Kim Gordon (Sonic Youth) performed on "School", Joan Jet performed on "Territorial Pissings", and Grohl's daughter Violet Grohl on "All Apologies". The performance is one of only a handful of reformations of the band following their breakup in 1994 following the Death of Kurt Cobain.[3]

February

[edit]
  • The 67th Annual Grammy Awards are held. Classic rock acts perform well; The Beatles win Best Rock Performance for the release of one of their final recordings to be released - "Now and Then", while The Rolling Stones win Best Rock Album for their first album in 18 years, Hackney Diamonds.[4]
  • St. Vincent wins three Grammy Awards - "Broken Man" wins Best Rock Song, "Flea" wins Best Alternative Music Performance, and All Born Screaming wins Best Alternative Music Album.[4]
  • Dream Theater releases their 16th studio album, Parasomnia. It is their first studio album since Black Clouds & Silver Linings (2009) with original drummer Mike Portnoy, who left the band in 2010, and rejoined the band in 2023.[5] It debuts in the top 10 of 13 separate national album charts.
  • Welsh rock band Manic Street Preachers releases their 15th studio album, Critical Thinking.[6] It debuts in the top 2 of the UK and Scottish album charts.
  • Japanese rock band One Ok Rock releases their eleventh studio album, Detox.[7] It debuts at number 2 of the Japanese national album charts.
  • English metalcore band Architects releases their eleventh studio album, The Sky, the Earth & All Between.[8] It was produced by former Bring Me the Horizon keyboardist Jordan Fish. It debuts in the top 10 of 6 separate national album charts.

March

[edit]
  • English rock band Jethro Tull releases their 24th studio album, Curious Ruminant.[9] It debuts in the top 5 of 3 separate national album charts.
  • English rock musician Steven Wilson (formerly of Porcupine Tree) releases his eighth solo studio album, The Overview. It is a concept album themed around the idea of the overview effect, consisting of two approximately 20 minute songs.[10][11] It debuts in the top 3 of 5 separate national album charts.
  • English hard rock band The Darkness releases their eighth studio album, Dreams on Toast. It is their first studio album since Motorheart (2021).[12] It debuts at number 2 on the UK and Scottish album charts.

Future releases

[edit]
  • Dutch symphonic metal band Epica is set to release their ninth studio album, Aspiral, on 11 April 2025.[13] They began working on it in March 2024.[14]
  • Magnolia Park is set to release their fourth studio album, a concept album titled Vamp, on April 11, 2025.[15]
  • Superheaven is set to release their third studio album, Superheaven, on April 18, 2025. It will be their first studio album in a decade, since Ours Is Chrome (2015).[16]
  • English heavy metal band Sleep Token are set to release their fourth studio album, Even in Arcadia, on 9 May 2025.[17] It will be their first since Take Me Back to Eden (2023).
  • Wavves is set to release their tenth studio album, Spun, on June 6, 2025, their first since Babes (2023).[18]
  • English rock band Hot Milk is set to release their second studio album, Corporation P.O.P, on 27 June 2025.[19]
  • Papa Roach is scheduled to release their twelfth studio album, their first since Ego Trip (2022). The band concurrently plans on celebrating the 25th anniversary of their breakout album Infest (2000) as well.[20]
  • Nine Inch Nails is scheduled to release new music, their first since the surprise dual release of Ghosts V and Ghosts VI (2020). At least some of the music was revealed to be part of Trent Reznor's efforts to score the 2025 film Tron: Ares.[21][22]
  • Mammoth WVH is scheduled to release their third studio album. Frontman Wolfgang Van Halen stated that work on the album began in January 2024, and the release is tentatively titled Mammoth III, completing the trilogy of release that started with Mammoth WVH (2021) and Mammoth II (2023).[23]
  • Shinedown is scheduled to release their eighth studio album, their first since Planet Zero (2022). Around mid-2024, the band revealed they had recorded six drum tracks for the album so far.[24]
  • Garbage is scheduled to release their eighth studio album, their first since No Gods No Masters (2021). Frontwoman Shirley Manson stated that the album may be their last and that they may be disbanding following its release.[25]
  • Motionless in White is scheduled to release their seventh studio album, their first since Scoring the End of the World (2022). The band is taking their time with its writing and recording, prioritizing quality over maintaining their momentum in popularity gains in the 2020s.[26]
  • Disturbed is scheduled to release their ninth studio album. In July 2024, guitarist Dan Donegan announced the band had begun work on recording a follow up to Divisive (2022).[27]
  • Five Finger Death Punch is scheduled to release their tenth studio album. In May 2024, guitarist Zoltan Bathory revealed the band had been recording a follow up to AfterLife (2022).[28]
  • Danish rock band Volbeat is set to release their ninth studio album. They started working on it after their 2023 Summer tour.[29] Frontman Michael Poulsen underwent a throat surgery in October 2023, which prevented the band from performing live shows in 2024.[30]
  • Anthrax is set to release their twelfth studio album, their first since For All Kings (2016).[31] Bassist Frank Bello said their upcoming album will be "heavier than ever".[32]
  • English rock band Def Leppard is set to release their 13th studio album. After releasing a single titled "Just Like '73", guitarist Phil Collen said it was "the seed of a new Def Leppard album".[33]
  • Halestorm is scheduled to release their sixth studio album.[34] The recording will emphasize recreating their live sound.[35][36]
  • French heavy metal band Gojira is set to release their eighth studio album. It will be their first studio album since Fortitude (2021), and since their live performance at the opening ceremony of the 2024 Summer Olympics in Paris, France.[37]
  • Welsh heavy metal band Bullet for My Valentine is set to release their eighth studio album, their first since their self-titled album (2021). They started working on it in 2024.[38]
  • Canadian rock band Three Days Grace is set to release their eighth studio album. It will be their first studio album since Transit of Venus (2012) with original frontman Adam Gontier, who left the band in 2013, and returned to the band in 2024. He was replaced by Matt Walst, who will also remain a lead singer as well, making them a two-vocal band.[39]
  • Slipknot band members are scheduled to release an album. The album, Look Outside Your Window, was recorded by four of the band's members in 2008 during the band's lengthy All Hope Is Gone sessions. The album will have a more experimental and melodic rock sound as opposed to their traditional metal sound.[40] The band once aimed to release it in 2023 once band members were scheduled from their record label contract.[41] It was delayed to 2024 due to Corey Taylor's commitment to his second solo album CMF2 in the second half of 2023, and then again to 2025.[42][43]
  • Slipknot is also set to release their eighth studio album. It will be their first studio album with drummer Eloy Casagrande, who replaced the band's longtime drummer Jay Weinberg in 2023.[44]
  • Canadian rock band Finger Eleven is set to release their eighth studio album, Adrenaline. It will be their first studio album in a decade, since Five Crooked Lines (2015).[45]
  • Blondie is set to release their twelfth studio album, their first since Pollinator (2017).[46]
  • English rock band The Cure is set to release their 15th studio album. It will be the follow-up album to Songs of a Lost World (2024).[47]
  • Korn plans to release their fifteenth studio album. Members have stated that they aim to meld their "vintage" sound with a "modern twist", and that it's going into a heavier direction. The band concurrently plans on celebrating the 30th anniversary of their first album, Korn (1994).[48][49]
  • Deftones are scheduled to release their tenth studio album. Work on the album began in early 2023, and will be their third album working with music producer Nick Raskulinecz.[50]
  • Australian progressive rock band Karnivool has recorded an album's worth of material, which would be its first release in over a decade. Work on material began as early as 2016, but has progressed slowly due to all band members growing older, starting families, and living in different areas now. The band hasn't decided if material will be released as a full album, or broken up across multiple EPs.[51][52]
  • The Pretty Reckless are scheduled to release their fifth studio album. Recording for the album began in mid-2023.[53]
  • Social Distortion is scheduled to release their eighth studio album. The band's follow-up to 2011's Hard Times and Nursery Rhymes was originally set for release in 2023, but vocalist and guitarist Mike Ness' diagnosis with stage one tonsil cancer delayed the album's release.[54]
  • Limp Bizkit is working on their seventh studio album. The band plans to work on new material during sound checks because their 2024 touring schedule is so heavy.[55]
  • Chevelle is set to release their tenth studio album. It was originally set to release in October 2024, but was delayed to 2025.[56]
  • Swedish rock band Ghost is scheduled to release their sixth studio album. Frontman Tobias Forge revealed that he had a few songs written for the album in late 2023.[57]
  • Former Rage Against the Machine guitarist Tom Morello is set to release his debut solo studio album.[58] It is his first album after the band abruptly disbanded in early January 2024.[59]
  • Breaking Benjamin is scheduled to release their seventh studio album, their first since 2018. Recording for the album began in either 2020 or 2021.[60]

Tours and festivals

[edit]
  • Sonic Temple festival is set to be held from May 8 to May 11, 2025. It will be headlined by Linkin Park, Metallica, and Korn, and will feature many other bands including Alice in Chains, Bullet for My Valentine, Bad Omens, and more.[61]
  • Welcome to Rockville is set to be held from May 15 to May 18, 2025. It will be headlined by Green Day, Linkin Park, Korn, and Shinedown, and will feature many other bands including Bowling for Soup, Three Days Grace, Taking Back Sunday, Real Friends, and more.[62]
  • Slam Dunk festival is set to be held on 24 and 25 May. It will be headlined by Neck Deep, Electric Callboy, and A Day to Remember, and will feature many other bands including Alkaline Trio, Hot Milk, Zebrahead, and more.[63]
  • Punk Rock Bowling Music Festival is set to be held from May 24, 2025, to May 26, 2025. It will be headlined by Social Distortion, Cock Sparrer, and Peter Hook & the Light, and will feature many other bands including Fidlar, The Damned, Power Trip, The Interrupters, and more.[64]
  • Download Festival is set to be held from 13 June to 15 June 2025. It will be headlined by Green Day, Korn, and Sleep Token, and will feature many other bands including Within Temptation, Sex Pistols, Steel Panther, Jimmy Eat World, and more.[65]
  • Warped Tour is set to return for the first time in six years to celebrate its 30th anniversary. Three festivals are set to take place in June, July, and November.[66]
  • English rock band Oasis reforms for the first time in 15 years for their Oasis Live '25 Tour. The announcement ends 15 years of bitter feuds between brothers Noel Gallagher and Liam Gallagher, who previously denied that any reunion would take effect.[67][68][69]
  • English heavy metal band Black Sabbath announces reunion with all four original members for one final concert. It is set take place at Villa Park in Birmingham, England, on 5 July 2025.[70]
  • Louder Than Life festival is set to take place from September 18 to September 21, 2025. It will be headlined by Slayer, Avenged Sevenfold, Deftones, and Bring Me the Horizon.[71]
  • Shaky Knees Music Festival is set to take place from September 19, to September 21, 2025. It will be headlined by blink-182, My Chemical Romance, and Deftones, and will feature many other bands including Soft Play, Devo, Franz Ferdinand, Pixies, and more.[72]
  • Aftershock Festival is set to take place from October 2, to October 5, 2025. It will be headlined by blink-182, Deftones, Korn, and Bring Me the Horizon.[73]
  • When We Were Young festival is set to be held on October 18, 2025. It will be headlined by blink-182, and Panic! at the Disco, and will feature many other bands including The Story So Far, Holding Absence, Sleeping with Sirens, Weezer, and more.[74]

Band breakups

[edit]
  • Australian metalcore band Void of Vision broke up following a final headline tour in Australia in February 2025.[75]
  • Canadian rock band Sum 41 broke up following the release of their final studio album Heaven :x: Hell (2024),[76] and a final worldwide headlining tour that ended in January 2025.[77] The band broke up after their induction in the Canadian Music Hall of Fame, and performed one final live performance at the 54th annual Juno Awards in March 2025.[78]
  • Brazilian heavy metal band Sepultura will break up after a farewell tour marking the band's 40th anniversary, set to end in September 2025.[79]
  • English rock band You Me at Six announced they will break up after over 20 years of performing following a final tour in 2025.[80][81]
  • X will break up following the release of their final studio album Smoke & Fiction (2024), with a final tour set to end in 2025.[82]

Deaths

[edit]
  • 6 January – Fredrik Lindgren, 53, Swedish death metal guitarist (Unleashed)[83]
  • 13 January – Peter "P. Fluid" Forrest, 64, American rock musician (24-7 Spyz)[84]
  • 20 January – John Sykes, 65, English rock guitarist (Whitesnake, Thin Lizzy, Tygers of Pan Tang)[85]
  • 21 January – Garth Hudson, 87, Canadian rock multi-instrumentalist (The Band)[86]
  • 29 January – Bruce Howe, 77, Australian progressive rock bassist and singer (Fraternity)[87]
  • 1 February – Sal Maida, 76, American rock bassist (Milk 'N' Cookies, Roxy Music, Sparks)[88]
  • 17 February – Rick Buckler, 69, English rock drummer (The Jam)[89]
  • 19 February – Snowy Fleet, 79, English-born Australian rock drummer (The Easybeats)[90]
  • 28 February – David Johansen, 75, American progressive rock singer (New York Dolls)[91]
  • 6 April – Clem Burke, 70, American rock drummer (Blondie)[92]
