This article lists events related to rail transport that occurred in 2025.

Events

[edit]

January

[edit]
  • January 1
    • – Line 5 of the Hangzhou Metro extends to East Nanhu from Jinxing.[1]
    • – Donghae Line of the Korail Network opens between Yeongdeok and Samcheok.[2]
  • January 2 – Bitong line of the Chongqing Suburban Railway opens between Bishan and Tongliangxi.[3]
  • January 3 – Baku-Balakən Route of the Azerbaijan Railways reopens between Baku and Balakən.[4]
  • January 5
    • – Magenta Line of the Delhi Metro extends to Krisna Park from Janakpuri West.[5]
    • – New Ashok Nagar To Meerut South Section of the Delhi–Meerut RRTS opens between New Ashok Nagar and Meerut South.[6]
    • – Line 3 of the Riyadh Metro opens between Jeddah Road and Khashm Al An.[7]
  • January 6
    • – O-Train: Line 2 reopens and extends to Limebank from Greenboro; Line 4 opens between Airport and South Keys.[8]
    • – Nice Tramway Network: Line B opens between Nice Côte d'Azur Airport Terminal 2 and CADAM; Line 3 is rerouted between Saint Isidore and Port Lympia.[9]
    • – Turqoise Line of the Lusail Tram opens between Rawdat Lusail and Crescent Park – North as a loop line.[10]
    • – Otogar–Opera Line of the Eskişehir Estram opens between Otogar and Opera.[11]
  • January 7 – Line 1 of the Tampere Light Rail extends to Lentävänniemi from Santalahti.[12]
  • January 10 – Line 4 of the Ningbo Metro extends to Cicheng West from Cicheng.[13]
  • January 11 – Gyooe Line of the Korail Network reopens between Daegok and Uijeongbu.[14]
  • January 18 – Villejuif–Gustave Roussy opens as an infill station of Paris Métro Line 14.[15]
  • January 19 – Chūō Line of the Osaka Metro extends to Yumeshima from Cosmosquare.[16]
  • January 20
    • – Kumanovo-Beljakovce Line of the Macedonia Railways reopens between Kumanovo and Beljakovce.[17]
    • – Southern Section of the Jungang Line opens between Cheongnyangni and Gyeongju.[18]
  • January 25
    • – Qingyuan Maglev Line opens between Yinzhan and Changlong.[19]
    • – Line T2 of the Florence Tramway Network extends to San Marco-Universita from Fortezza.[20]
    • – Line D of the Tunis TFR opens between Tunis and Gobâa.[21]
  • January 27
    • – Line 9 of the Sao Paulo Metro extends to Varginha from Mendes-Vila Natal.[22]
    • – Cuneo-Savigliano Line of the Arenaways reopens between Cuneo and Savigliano.[23]

February

[edit]
  • February 1 – Trieste–Opicina Tramway reopens between Piazza Oberdan and Villa Opicina.[24]
  • February 3 – Finnish Main Line of the Finnish Railways is electrified between Hyvinkää and Karis.[25]
  • February 5 – Brussels-Venice Route of the European Sleeper opens between Brussels and Venice.[26]
  • February 8 – Trenitalia introduces new passenger services between Venice and Nova Gorica.[27]
  • February 9 – Leixões Line of the Comboios Railway reopens between Contumil and Leixões.[28]
  • February 11 – Georgian Railway resumes passenger services between Tbilisi and Ozurgeti.[29]
  • February 12 – Line 1 of the Lijiang Rail Transit (Lijiang Snow Mountain Sightseeing Train) opens between Youkezhongxin and Yulongxueshan.[30]
  • February 15 – The New Delhi railway station crowd crush, in which at least 18 people lost their lives.
  • February 20 – Haniska pri Košiciach – Moldava nad Bodvou Line is electrified between Haniska pri Kosiciach and Moldava nad Bodvou.[31]
  • February 22 – Line 1 of the Taiyuan Metro opens between Helongwan and Wusu 1 Hao / 2 Hao Hangzhanlou.[32]
  • February 27 – Jerusalem Light Rail extends to Neve Yaakov from Heil Ha-Avir; also extends to Hadassah Ein Kerem from Mount Herzl.[33][34]

March

[edit]
  • March 2 – Line T1 of the Luxembourg Tramway Network extends to Luxembourg Airport-Findel from Luxexpo.[35]
  • March 3 – Passenger services resume on part of the Moselle Valley Railway [de] after over 40 years.[36]
  • March 9 – Passenger services resume between Tehran and Van.[37]
  • March 15 – Extension of KTM ETS services to Segamat from Gemas.[38][39][40]
  • March 23 – Line T14 of the Ile-de-France Tramway Network opens between Esbly and Crécy-la-Chapelle.[41]
  • March 24 – As part of the South Coast Rail project, the Fall River/New Bedford Line of the MBTA opens from Boston to New Bedford and Fall River.[42]
  • March 28 – Tashkent-Xoʻjakent Railway is extended from Xoʻjakent to Chinorkent.[43]
  • March 31 – The Culoz–Modane railway and the Turin–Modane railway reopen between Saint-Jean-de-Maurienne and Oulx following a 2023 landslide.[44][45]

April

[edit]
  • April 1
    • – Line S61 of Esko Prague opens between Praha-Vršovice and Úvaly.[46]
    • – Line 1 of the Naples Metro extends to Centro Direzionale from Garibaldi.[47]
    • – Keisei acquires the Shin-Keisei Railway and its lone route is renamed to the Matsudo Line.[48]
    • – Nankai acquires the Semboku Rapid Railway and its lone route is renamed to the Semboku Line.[49]
  • April 4 – La Dolce Vita Orient Express launches, starting between Rome and Montalcino.[50]

Predicted and scheduled events

[edit]

April

[edit]
  • April 10 – Line 3 of the Mumbai Metro extends to Acharya Atrey Chowk from Income Tax Office.[51][52]
  • April 15 – Liege Tramway Network opens between Sclessin and Coronmeuse.[53]
  • April 19 – Katra To Banihal Section of the Jammu–Baramulla Line opens between Katra and Banihal.[54]
  • April 30 – Line 7 of the Changchun Rail Transit opens between Qiche Gongyuan and East Ring Road.[55]
  • – Yellow Line of the Chochabamba Metropolitan Train opens between Estación Central San Antonio and El Castillo.[56]
  • – Delhi–Alwar RRTS to be completed, allowing speeds up to 160 kilometres per hour (99 mph) between Delhi and Alwar.[57]
  • – Yellow Line of the Namma Metro opens between Bommasandra-Hosa Road and Bommasandra.[58]
  • – Line 3 of the Madrid Metro extends to El Casar from Villaverde Alto.[59]
  • – Avelia Liberty trains to replace Acela Express trains along the Northeast Corridor.[60]
  • – South Wales Metro: Coryton Line is electrified between Coryton and Cardiff Central; Rhymney Line is electrified between Caerphilly and Cardiff Central; Vale of Glamorgan Line is electrified between Penarth and Cardiff Central.[61][62]

[edit]

  • – Line 3 of the Mumbai Metro extends to Cuffe Parade from Acharya Atrey Chowk.[65]
  • – West Lake Corridor of the South Shore Line opens between Chicago and Dyer.[66]
