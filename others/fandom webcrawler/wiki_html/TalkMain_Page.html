Welcome! This page is for discussing the contents of the English Wikipedia's Main Page.
For general questions unrelated to the Main Page, please visit the Teahouse or check the links below.
To add content to an article, edit that article's page.
Irrelevant posts on this page may be removed.
Click here to report errors on the Main Page.

If you have a question related to the Main Page, please search the talk page archives first to check if it has previously been addressed:


For questions about using and contributing to the English Wikipedia:
  • To submit an article or a redirect, please use the article wizard.
  • The Teahouse: to ask your first basic questions about contributing to Wikipedia
  • The help desk: to ask technical questions about using Wikipedia (e.g. how to edit, images, categories)
  • The village pump: to ask questions about Wikipedia policies, guidelines or operations
  • The reference desk: to ask questions about subjects other than Wikipedia (including topics covered in its articles)
  • The administrators' noticeboard: to report a problem (e.g. vandalism)
  • Question help: to find out more about where to ask questions or make comments on Wikipedia
To suggest content for a Main Page section:
  • Main Page errors: to report errors in current or upcoming Main Page content
  • Today's featured article requests: to suggest a featured article for "Today's featured article"
  • In the news candidates: to nominate an item for "In the news"
  • Did you know suggestions: to nominate an item for "Did you know"
  • Selected anniversaries: to add an anniversary to "On this day"
  • Today's featured list submissions: to suggest a featured list for "Today's featured list"
  • Featured picture candidates: images appearing as picture of the day are selected roughly according to the order in which they were promoted to featured status
  • Wikipedia languages: to propose a change to the list of Wikipedias in other languages
  • Main Page frequently asked questions: to learn more about the Main Page

  • Where is the error? An exact quotation of the text in question helps.
  • Offer a correction if possible.
  • References are helpful, especially when reporting an obscure factual or grammatical error.
  • Time zones. The Main Page runs on Coordinated Universal Time (UTC, currently 00:40 on 8 April 2025) and is not adjusted to your local time zone.
  • Can you resolve the problem yourself? If the error lies primarily in the content of an article linked from the Main Page, fix the problem there before reporting it here. Text on the Main Page generally defers to the articles with bolded links. Upcoming content on the Main Page is usually only protected from editing beginning 24 hours before its scheduled appearance. Before that period, you can be bold and fix any issues yourself.
  • Do not use {{edit fully-protected}} on this page, which will not get a faster response. It is unnecessary, because this page is not protected, and causes display problems. (See the bottom of this revision for an example.)
  • No chit-chat. Lengthy discussions should be moved to a suitable location elsewhere, such as the talk page of the relevant article or project.
  • Respect other editors. Another user wrote the text you want changed, or reported an issue they see in something you wrote. Everyone's goal should be producing the best Main Page possible. The compressed time frame of the Main Page means sometimes action must be taken before there has been time for everyone to comment. Be civil to fellow users.
  • Reports are removed when resolved. Once an error has been addressed or determined not to be an error, or the item has been rotated off the Main Page, the report will be removed from this page. Check the revision history for a record of any discussion or action taken; no archives are kept.

Featured article and ‘did you know?’

STALE
Discussion about Did you know? articles and Today's featured articles relating to American pop culture on the main page has gone stale. This is a matter of Systemic bias on Wikipedia (see also Systemic bias on Wikipedia), and this is probably the wrong forum. Pending archiving within 24 hours. —Sparkle and Fade (talk • contributions) 05:47, 7 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]

The following discussion is closed. Please do not modify it. Subsequent comments should be made on the appropriate discussion page. No further edits should be made to this discussion.

Is there any particular reason why the items in the ‘did you know?’ section and the featured articles almost invariably relate to American popular culture? Do a few editors make a majority of the suggestions? It’s tiresome drivel. Today’s (1 April) are especially vapid. Keeper of Albion (talk) 00:59, 1 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]

Keeper of Albion: DYK displays what gets nominated and is eligible. If there's a bias, then please provide your own nominations to balance things out.
The story with featured articles is somewhat different. There is a limited pool of pages that haven't been shown yet (last time I looked, there were just over 700 articles yet to be featured). The coordinators can only choose from that pool, and every FA will eventually run. Once again, if you detect a bias, simply bring other topics up to FA status and thus, you can do something about it. Schwede66 02:32, 1 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Also, nominations from the pool of articles that we have are always welcome at WP:TFA/R. If you write a blurb and nominate an eligible article it is one less blurb we have to write. Wehwalt (talk) 11:38, 1 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Today's DYK entries include a Spanish anarchist group, a New Zealand horse, an Indonesian politician, an Australian election, and a Neptunian broadcast. jlwoodwa (talk) 03:38, 1 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
Yes @Keeper of Albion. I have suggested creating a section with random articles (with some criteria, of course; they should at least be representative of the encyclopedic content we’re aiming for). Because the gap is huge, we will need some years before the encyclopedia can achieve balance in featured and good articles as @Schwede66 explained. This way, we could have a good rotation of articles somehow less baised, in my opinion. Regards Riad Salih (talk) 04:24, 1 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
We already have the random article button. --User:Khajidha (talk) (contributions) 16:22, 3 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
The button you're referring to is different. It leads to random articles, where the content quality is not checked, and they do not appear on the main page, so no visibility. Riad Salih (talk) 02:03, 4 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
The time and effort you would expend on checking the articles for minimum quality (which would have to be defined and standardized first) is more than the benefit to the readers that your idea would produce compared to them just clicking random article until they find something they are intrigued by. --User:Khajidha (talk) (contributions) 14:52, 4 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]

The OP mentioned April 1 without mentioning recognition of Wikipedia:April Fools' Main Page. Art LaPella (talk) 00:45, 4 April 2025 (UTC)[reply]
